Time Management Tools

Use planners and matrices to manage study time wisely. Try the Eisenhower Matrix to prioritize tasks:

  • Urgent & Important: Do it now
  • Important but Not Urgent: Plan it
  • Urgent but Not Important: Delegate it
  • Neither: Eliminate it

Try blocking 1-2 hours each day just for math review.

Note-Taking Methods

Cornell Notes: Divide the page into three parts – Notes, Cues, and Summary.

Mind Mapping: Visual method to connect ideas – great for formulas and problem types.

Outlining: Organize notes in bullet points with headings and subpoints.

Memorization Techniques

  • Mnemonics: Use phrases like PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, etc.)
  • Spaced Repetition: Review material over increasing intervals
  • Flashcards: Use tools like Quizlet for key terms and formulas

Exam Preparation & Procrastination Tips

  • Use the Pomodoro Technique: 25-minute focus, 5-minute break
  • Study actively: solve problems, teach others, quiz yourself
  • Limit distractions: silence your phone and stay off social media
